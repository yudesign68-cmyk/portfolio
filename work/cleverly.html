Case Study · 2024

Cleverly

AI-Powered Learning Platform — designing an interface that helps university students confidently integrate AI into their academic workflows.

UX Research Product Design Interaction Design Visual Design

Role

Lead UI Designer, UX Contributor

Team

4 UX/UI Designers

Context

Monash University Capstone

Output

High-fidelity Interactive Prototype

Background

Building AI literacy for the next generation of students.

This project was developed as a conceptual capstone project at Monash University in collaboration with the Student Academic Success (SAS) team. Cleverly sought to inspire students to actively engage with AI — not just as a tool, but as a learning companion. The goal was to enhance AI literacy, equip students with suitable AI tools, and promote ethical AI use — ultimately preparing students to confidently integrate AI into their academic and professional careers.

Our design approach followed a lean and iterative UX process, prioritising usability testing and rapid prototyping. While this case study includes the UX process for context, the main focus is on the UI iteration process, where my contributions were most impactful.

The Problem

University students struggle to integrate AI tools into their academic workflows.

AI is reshaping how we work and make decisions every day. While it's becoming a staple in many industries, students often find it challenging to use AI effectively in their studies. Instead of being a fully integrated learning partner, AI is frequently used in a limited way — missing out on its full potential.

Research

Why do students find it tough to embrace AI?

Due to project constraints, we conducted secondary research and affinity mapping, identifying three key barriers to effective AI-driven learning.

Snapshots from our affinity mapping and market research process.

Challenge Insight
Overwhelming Information Rapid AI evolution makes it hard for students to stay updated and choose the right tools.
Limited Time for Exploration AI tools often lack differentiation, leading to decision paralysis. Students abandon tools with complex onboarding.
Ethical & Policy Concerns Uncertainty about university policies makes students hesitant to use AI.

Target User

Meet Emmy, a university student embarking on a new semester.

To ensure our design truly addressed student needs, we developed a user persona and storyboard — grounded in research insights and our own experiences as students. This process helped us step into Emmy's shoes, deepening our empathy and shaping solutions that tackle real challenges.

Snapshots of persona development and storyboard.

By analysing Emmy's experience, we identified key insights to guide our design decisions.

  • Lack of AI literacyFinds AI concepts confusing and lacks confidence in applying them academically.
  • Limited timeStudents have limited time and struggle to efficiently find and learn the right AI tools.
  • Ethical concernsUncertainty about AI policies makes students hesitant and unsure about their usage.

Goals & Needs

  • Build AI knowledgeWants simple, structured learning to build AI knowledge without feeling overwhelmed.
  • Guided selectionNeeds guided recommendations to select suitable AI tools with step-by-step interactive guidance.

"How might we design an intuitive AI learning system that inspires students to actively engage with AI while fostering responsible use and building strong AI literacy?"

Feature Definition

Aligning student needs with client expectations.

We identified students' core challenges — but we also needed to align with the client's expectations. The SAS team prioritised AI literacy, interactive learning, and personalised AI adoption. To bridge both, we created a feature definition table as our roadmap before moving to the user flow.

Feature definition table mapping user challenges to platform features.

BiteWise

AI-driven curation of bite-sized AI-related external content — tackling information overload and keeping students updated without feeling overwhelmed.

LearnLab

AI-powered resource recommendations and interactive workspace for hands-on AI learning — enabling guided exploration of tools directly applied to real assignments.

CleverMate

AI-powered assistant offering real-time AI tool guidance and responsible AI usage support — addressing ethical concerns and providing academic integrity assurance.

CleverLog

AI learning tracker that analyses platform engagement and refines learning paths based on user activity — enabling students to monitor progress and reflect on their journey.

UI Iteration

Three phases of design refinement.

With Cleverly's core features defined, the next step was crafting a visual identity that reinforced its mission. For students to embrace AI, the design needed to instil confidence — a structured, intuitive interface that eliminates hesitation and makes AI learning feel seamless.

Phase 1 — We thought we had a solid direction, until we saw it in action.

Problem

Our initial design lacked confidence and engagement. The muted blue palette and text-heavy interface made the platform feel uninspiring for students.

Solution

Introduced a vibrant orange palette to create a more energetic feel. Designed a dynamic layout to establish a stronger visual hierarchy.

Phase 2 — The bigger the project got, the harder it was to keep things consistent.

Problem

As the project expanded, inconsistencies in spacing, typography, and colour usage made design cohesion difficult. Without a structured system, collaboration became inefficient.

Solution

Developed a comprehensive design system with standardised UI components. Ensured WCAG-compliant contrast ratios. Built a Figma component library to streamline collaboration.

Phase 3 — We were nearly there, but the brand identity still wasn't clear.

Problem

Despite previous refinements, the brand identity still lacked clarity and structure, making the platform feel less distinctive.

Solution

Introduced a structured line motif to enhance brand consistency. Redesigned the layout for visual clarity. Implemented animated interactions to increase engagement.

Usability Testing

We went outside, engaged with real target users, and listened.

To validate our design decisions, we conducted task-based usability testing with a think-aloud approach. 4 students from different academic backgrounds completed real-world scenarios while verbalising their thoughts, with interactions observed and recorded.

Based on these insights, we identified how to iterate.

Issue Identified Design Refinement
Navigation Clarity
Low icon visibility made it difficult for users to interact with main page cards.		 Increased icon contrast and size to improve visibility.
Content Readability
Text blocks reduced scannability and approachability.		 Reorganised information with clearer typography and spacing.
Colour Overload
Overuse of vivid colours made it hard for users to focus on content.		 Adjusted colour balance to prioritise readability and reduce distractions.

Visual System

We refined the details to create a more cohesive experience.

To reinforce a cohesive brand presence, we developed a final visual system ensuring UI consistency across all components. The style guide served as a centralised reference — minimising inconsistencies, improving collaboration, and streamlining the design process.

To ensure successful adoption, I conducted a training session for team members, explaining how to apply the style guide effectively. This helped standardise future designs and maintain visual coherence across all platforms.

Final Design

A platform that makes AI learning feel natural and approachable.

Cleverly provides an AI-powered learning experience guided by two key visual principles — Confidence and Approachability — balancing bold, structured elements with an intuitive, engaging interface to encourage AI exploration without hesitation.

BiteWise — delivers bite-sized AI insights so students stay updated without feeling overwhelmed.

LearnLab — guided workspace for applying AI tools directly to real assignments.

CleverLog — enables students to monitor progress, set goals, and reflect on their learning journey.

CleverMate — real-time AI guidance for ethical and effective AI use.

Reflections

What I learned along the way.

Collaboration & Communication

Design is never a solo effort. Hosting a style system workshop helped align the team, streamline decision-making, and create a shared visual language. Pitching ideas to stakeholders sharpened my ability to communicate design rationale clearly and persuasively.

Embracing Iteration

Initially, it was daunting to revisit and change core design elements — but we learned that good design requires bold iteration. Sometimes, the best decision is to step back, reassess, and refine, even if it means reworking foundational elements.

Less is More

At the start, our team wanted to showcase everything, but the result was overwhelming. This project taught me the importance of focus and clarity — prioritising what truly matters to create a more intuitive user experience.

Bridging AI & Design

Rather than treating AI as a magic solution, we conducted in-depth research to understand its real capabilities and limitations. This informed our design approach, ensuring we leveraged AI realistically.
